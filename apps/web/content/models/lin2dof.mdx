## At a glance

- Captures coupled lateral velocity and yaw-rate dynamics with constant longitudinal speed.
- Linear tyre model (mu constant) keeps the equations analytically friendly.
- Useful for stability margin studies, controller frequency-response checks, and quick yaw-gain estimates.
- Omits nonlinear slip saturation and roll, so limit handling must be validated elsewhere.

## Equations

States: $, $, $\psi$, $, $ with constant forward speed $. Steering input is the front axle steer $\delta$.

$$
m (\dot{v_y} + v r) = C_f (\delta - (v_y + a r)/v) + C_r (-(v_y - b r)/v)
I_z \dot{r} = a C_f (\delta - (v_y + a r)/v) - b C_r (-(v_y - b r)/v)
\dot{\psi} = r
\dot{x} = v \cos \psi - v_y \sin \psi
\dot{y} = v \sin \psi + v_y \cos \psi
$$

Outputs include yaw rate $, lateral acceleration  \approx \dot{v_y} + v r$, and sideslip $\beta = \arctan2(v_y, v)$.

## Assumptions & limits

- Small slip angles (linear tyre approximation).
- Constant speed, no roll or heave dynamics.
- Tyre properties lumped into single cornering stiffness per axle.

## Canonical scenarios

1. **5 deg step steer at 25 m/s** -- baseline yaw-rate gain and damping check.
2. **Frequency sweep** -- optional manual scenario to examine under/over-steer trends.
3. **Parameter sweep** -- vary Cf/Cr to visualize stability margin badges.
